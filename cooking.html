About

Why Korean Food?

Growing up bilingual in a Korean household means food was always a language of its own. From the sizzle of bulgogi on a hot grill to the slow patience of fermenting kimchi, Korean cooking taught me that the best meals take time, intention, and love.

This page is a small celebration of the dishes, ingredients, and traditions that make Korean cuisine one of the most vibrant and soulful in the world.

Korean Kitchen Essentials

Every great Korean cook keeps these staples on hand — they're the backbone of the cuisine's layered, fermented flavor.

🌶️

Gochugaru

Korean red pepper flakes — the soul of kimchi and countless stews. Smoky, fruity, and warming.

🫙

Doenjang

Fermented soybean paste, rich and pungent. The Korean answer to miso, only bolder.

🧄

Garlic

Used in almost everything. Korean cuisine uses more garlic per capita than nearly any other cuisine.

🍶

Sesame Oil

A finishing touch that transforms a dish. A few drops of toasted sesame oil elevates everything.

🥢

Ganjang

Korean soy sauce — slightly sweeter and more complex than Japanese varieties. Essential for marinades.

🌿

Perilla Leaves

Fragrant herb used as a wrap or garnish. Minty, anise-like, and unlike anything else.

Must-Try Korean Dishes

A ranked list of essential Korean dishes — each one a world of flavor on its own.

  1. Kimchi (김치)
    • Baechu-kimchi — classic napa cabbage, the most iconic
    • Kkakdugi — cubed radish kimchi, crunchy and refreshing
    • Oi sobagi — stuffed cucumber kimchi, perfect in summer
  2. Bibimbap (비빔밥)
    • Mixed rice bowl topped with seasoned vegetables, egg, and gochujang
    • Dolsot bibimbap — served in a sizzling stone pot for crispy rice
  3. Bulgogi (불고기)
    • Thinly sliced marinated beef grilled over flame
    • Marinade: soy sauce, pear, sesame, garlic, ginger
    • Wraps beautifully in perilla or lettuce leaves
  4. Tteokbokki (떡볶이)
    • Chewy rice cakes in a spicy-sweet gochujang sauce
    • Popular street food, often served with fish cakes and boiled eggs
  5. Samgyeopsal (삼겹살)
    • Thick slices of pork belly grilled at the table
    • Eaten wrapped in lettuce with garlic, ssamjang, and kimchi
